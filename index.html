Human Awareness for Mobile Robot Navigation on a Resource-Constrained Jetson Nano

IEEE 27th International Multi Topic Conference (INMIC) 2025

This research presents a navigation framework that enables mobile robots to safely move around people by considering both their location and movement. Designed for resource-constrained platforms like the NVIDIA Jetson Nano, the system combines LiDAR-based leg detection with depth-camera data to accurately locate humans in real time. Built on the Robot Operating System (ROS), it dynamically updates costmaps, allowing the robot to adjust its path while maintaining smooth motion through PID control. Various optimizations were implemented to ensure efficient use of limited processing power and memory. Real-world indoor testing demonstrated reliable human detection and navigation with minimal delay and negligible impact on the robot’s movement. This framework provides a practical solution for integrating human-aware navigation into social, service, and assistive robots on compact hardware.
